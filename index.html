Mathematical Background

The Sierpiński problem (1964) asks: Are there infinitely many positive integers that cannot be expressed in any of the four forms:

  • 6ab + a + b
  • 6ab + a - b
  • 6ab - a + b
  • 6ab - a - b

These forms arise from the modular lattice projection: (3a ± 1)(2b ± 1) - 1 where a must be even to ensure factors remain in visible residue classes {1,5} mod 6.

The problem remains unsolved - we don't know if the set of uncovered numbers is finite or infinite.
